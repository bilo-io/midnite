# Office

## What it is

Office is a **pixel-art floor** of your agents — a spatial, game-like take on the
fleet. Each agent has a desk, and the room is driven by **live presence**, so the
scene changes as work starts and stops:

- **Desks** map one-to-one to your agents.
- **Presence** lights up a desk when its agent is active, so a busy floor is a busy
  fleet — no list to parse.
- **Walking up** to a desk is how you reach that agent: call it, or drop into its
  live [session](/agents/sessions).

Office is **desktop-only** — it's gated behind the desktop breakpoint, since the
floor needs room to move around in.

## Purpose

Office is the playful counterpart to the [board](/app/tasks) and
[Sessions](/agents/sessions). Where those are lists and terminals, Office is a
room you can move through — a quick, human way to answer "who's working right
now?" and to reach a specific agent by walking over to it rather than hunting
through a table. It's the same fleet, seen spatially.

## Using it

| Action | How | Behind the scenes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Walk the floor | Move around the room | presence-driven view |
| Switch views | View selector (`?view=`) | office views |
| Call / message an agent | Step up to its desk | presence + session |
| See who's active | Live presence on the floor | `getPresenceSummary` |

> Office requires a desktop-width viewport; on smaller screens it's hidden behind
> the desktop-only gate.
