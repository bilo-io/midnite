# Digests

## What it is

**Fleet digests** are periodic roll-ups of what shipped, what failed, and what
needs attention across your workspace. Each digest is a generated snapshot of a
time window — think of it as an automatic changelog for your agents:

- **Shipped** — the tasks that completed and the pull requests that merged.
- **Failed** — runs that errored or were abandoned, so nothing slips silently.
- **Needs attention** — the follow-ups waiting on you.

The feed is **mostly read-only**: digests are produced on a cadence, not authored
by hand, and each links back to the [tasks](/app/tasks) and
[sessions](/agents/sessions) behind every line.

> **Where to find it.** Digests live on the **Digest tab of [Ops](/overview/ops)**
> (`/ops?tab=digest`) rather than a standalone sidebar entry — they sit alongside
> the rest of your fleet-health analytics. Old `/digests` links still resolve and
> redirect there.

## Purpose

Digests answer "**what happened while I was away?**" in one place. Instead of
reconstructing activity by scanning the [board](/app/tasks) and every
[session](/agents/sessions), you read the summary — the wins, the failures, and
the follow-ups — and drill in only where you need to. Export one to Markdown when
you want to drop a status update into a doc, a PR, or a standup.

## Listing & viewing

The tab shows the most recent digests first. Select one to read its full
structured summary, then follow the deep links back to the work behind it.

| Action | How | Behind the scenes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| List digests | Ops → **Digest** tab | `getDigests` |
| Open a digest | Select one (`?id=`) | `getDigest` |
| Export as Markdown | Digest menu → **Export** | `exportDigest` |

> Because digests are generated on a cadence rather than created by hand, this is
> a feed to **read and export**, not a CRUD surface — there's nothing to create or
> edit. To change *how* the fleet reports, look to the runs it summarises on the
> [board](/app/tasks) and in [Ops](/overview/ops).
